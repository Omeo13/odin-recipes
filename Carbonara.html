Carbonara

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper.

It's creamy, rich, and incredibly satisfying. The key to a perfect carbonara is to mix the hot pasta with the egg mixture quickly to create a silky sauce without scrambling the eggs.

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions. Reserve some pasta water and drain the rest.
  2. In a skillet, cook the pancetta over medium heat until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and cheese until well combined.
  4. Add the hot pasta to the skillet with pancetta and remove from heat.
  5. Quickly pour the egg mixture over the pasta, tossing to combine. Add reserved pasta water as needed to create a creamy sauce.
  6. Season with salt and black pepper. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Tips

  • Use high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • Serve immediately for the creamiest texture.
  • Pair with a side salad and garlic bread for a complete meal.
  • Store leftovers in the fridge for up to 2 days.
